Corona Diary (WIP)

Elliot Z. Lin

2020年 3月 18日

Today I lifted weights in the morning. I had mixed feelings because I felt good getting back into my morning gym routine but frustrated because my form was still nagging me. No matter.

Work today was productive, but I felt more resistance than I did yesterday. Maybe the euphoria of working from home is wearing off. I hope I can keep up my focus for the coming weeks. That would be disappointing if I had to admit to myself I couldn't focus at home, despite my best efforts at maintaining a routine and dressing up for work.

After work I DoorDashed for a bit before making a Costco run. I did good. Despite dashing for only 1.25 hours and making only 3 deliveries, I made more money tonight than I did on any of my other nights. Bless you customers who tipped me so generously! I don't deserve it, but you gave me the benefit of the doubt regardless. Other thoughts: traffic was really light on the streets, which was extra nice. It's rare I get to drive around town in the evening twilight in such peace. I think I'll do more DoorDashing in the coming weeks.

I went to Costco to pick up some eggs and salted butter. I came home with a whole cartload of food for the coming weeks. Come on now, I was buying for my brother too! On a similar note to my DoorDash story, it was also incredibly empty at the Costco, emptier than on regular weekdays. What a treat!

2020年 3月 31日

We have a little over a week until April 7, the final day of our shelter-in-place order. However there was already talk of extending it to May 1. Trump himself had recommended over the weekend and the 6 Bay Area counties were preparing to make their announcement.

Later on we find out that it's actually extended to May 3. That it was extended past April 7 surprised none of us. Personally, I was holding out some hope that it wouldn't go past May 3, but I'm still not willing to put money on that.

None of this mattered much to me at the moment because I was working hard on getting a web release out. We had a sort of self-imposed deadline of April 1 and we were working our tail ends off to push as many features as we could by then. The three of us, my boss, a coworker, and I worked until about 12:30am, with all communications over a Zoom call. Afterwards my coworker signed off -- he was on the East Coast after all and was up criminally late -- and my boss and I sallied forth before calling it a day at 4:30am.

It was about the most fun I've had since the first couple of days of shelter-in-place.

2020年 4月 1日

I had an incredible itch to scratch this day. After burning the midnight oil several nights in a row, my boss deemd it fitting to give me and my aforementioned coworker the day off to rest. He probably needed it more than I did but I gladly took my share of the credit.

At around that time I was getting hooked on watching cooking shows on TV and on the Internet and fantasized building my home kitchen. I couldn't resist it. Then I was a college student with no income -- I did have an allowance, but I would've felt guilt spending that money on anything other than the bare essentials, in my case rent, food, and tuition. Now I had decent money, and I was still employed amidst this coronavirus scare.

Instead of resting, I dashed out of the house to pick up some more food. Might as well stock up some more while I had time off on a weekday so I could comfortably ride out my self-imposed quarantine until May 3. After 3 hours running about town, I came home with my modest (compared to the other shoppers I saw) haul, which included a 25 lb sack of "H&R" All-Purpose Flour. H&R stands for "Hotel and Restaurant". What else would you expect from Costco? Apparently it was no different from regular all-purpose flour you'd find in a grocery store. It was just marketed towards commercial use. I can just pretend I'm a home cook with a large-scale operation. I'll wear that proudly.

One interesting tidbit I gleaned from these times is Amazon can't be relied upon for a fair amount of items. You were better off driving to a brick-and-mortar store if you wanted to pick up water bottles or toilet paper than you were trying to order off Amazon. You could forget about ordering groceries off of Amazon Fresh because there were no delivery times available. And darn it, all the instant dry yeast online was marked up at least 100%. In the middle of my cooking reverie I bought a stand mixer and a Dutch oven. I had to purhcase both from their respective manufacturers' websites because they were out of stock on Amazon. Did I over-exaggerate my claim at the beginning of this paragraph? Yes. But in truth you could really see Amazon buckling under all this pressure.

2020年 4月 4日

Today I made pasta the right way. Granted it was only spaghetti with some marinara sauce. But still, this time around I followed the best practices I could find on the Internet as closely as I could manage. I heated my sauce beforehand; cooked with tongs so as to more easily transfer the pasta noodles (almost losing 2 strands in the process); I cooked the pasta with the sauce instead of pouring the sauce over the noodles at the end; I properly doused the pasta and sauce with my pasta water, instead of simply adding pasta water to my sauce all by itself, which gave me sad, water sauce. And what a difference it made! Indeed the sauce and pasta had become one. With every bite I tasted the wonderfully rich Newman's Own marinara sauce (still working up the courage to rolling my own batch) followed by the al dente chew of the Barilla spaghetti noodles. And when I had finished with my pasta, my plate was actually mostly clean. Okay, perhaps I thickened my sauce too much, but still, this was the first time I had seen such a stark difference between the right and wrong ways of cooking something. My mind was actually blown by this experience.

You can really hear the difference between listening to it from your front porch vs listening to it from within a sunroom (or any glass enclosure).
